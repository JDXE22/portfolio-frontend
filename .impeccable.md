## Design Context

### Users
A mixed audience of recruiters, hiring managers, potential clients, and fellow developers. They arrive to evaluate David's experience and technical capabilities, browse his project portfolio, and assess whether he's a good fit for roles or collaborations. The site must serve both quick-scan evaluators (recruiters spending 30 seconds) and deeper explorers (developers reviewing project details).

### Brand Personality
**Clean, confident, modern.** The portfolio should communicate competence and professionalism without feeling corporate or sterile. It should feel like a developer who cares about craft — both in code and presentation.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Dark mode primary with full light mode support. Cool-toned palette anchored by teal-blue (#296e85) as a potential primary accent, alongside the existing Malibu blue system.
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode as first-class experiences (not just a media query fallback).
- **Anti-references**: None specified — but the direction should avoid generic AI-generated portfolio aesthetics.
- **Color exploration**: Open to evolving the palette around #296e85 as a deeper, more sophisticated anchor color.

### Design Principles
1. **Substance over flash** — Let the work speak. Every visual choice should support content, not compete with it.
2. **Scannable depth** — Support both 30-second evaluations and deep dives. Key information surfaces immediately; detail is available on demand.
3. **Confident restraint** — Bold enough to be memorable, restrained enough to feel professional. No gratuitous effects.
4. **Craft signals craft** — The quality of the portfolio itself demonstrates the quality of the developer. Every detail matters.
